Kandinsky, W. (1923). Composition 8 [Oil on canvas].

Wassily Kandinsky, a pioneer of Western abstract art, masterfully explored the expressive potential of visual elements stripped of representational content. In this composition, he uses points, lines, and planes not to depict objects, but to evoke rhythm, emotion, and harmony. The work exemplifies how abstract arrangements can communicate complex ideas and feelings through pure form and spatial relationships.
