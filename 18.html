Ishihara, S. (1917). Plate No. 1 [Watercolor on paper].

This image is from the Ishihara Color Test, created by Japanese ophthalmologist Shinobu Ishihara in 1917 to detect red–green color deficiencies in military recruits. Originally hand-painted in watercolor, the plates use carefully arranged points of color to reveal — or obscure — hidden figures, showing how perception depends on both biology and design.
