ART AND SPIN

Music and lyrics by Clarice DuVall & Jimmy “Buzz” McCallister

Well now...
You say “design,” I say “art”
They say “obey,” and I say “depart!”
We all look on, but not the same—
The work is often defined by its frame...

Is it art, craft, propaganda or design?
Is yours the right answer, or is it mine?
The question to ask again and again:
Is it freeing your heart—or tightening the chain?

Art stirs the soul with a whisper of doubt,
Design shines a light, helps you find a way out.
Propaganda shouts with a chest full of pride—
"We know what’s better! It is us who decide!"

Art wants you free, with your mind on a flight,
Design wants you comfy, the couch just feels right.
Propaganda is fueled by fear and by blame
“Want to be safe? Throw a book to the flame!”

From cathedrals to canvas, to code in a screen,
What we see isn't always what’s seen.
That chapel? Divine! Or an altar to spin?
Depends on who’s praying, depends on who wins?

Art takes a stand, gives the voiceless a voice,
Design smooths the ride, gives the user a choice.
But propaganda pounds like a drum in the night—
“March to the tune, don’t question what’s right!”

So next time you’re dazzled by media, so fine.
Ask: Who penned the slogan? Who drew the line?
And these parting words—I’ll sing with a grin:
Even this little tune...
Could be art, could be spin.
